Visualizations


On this page, we look at each of the four weather attributes in a little more depth. On each tab, you will find an overall view and separate views for the Northern and Southern Hemispheres. The weather conditions were all recorded on October 31, 2022 and all the data was provided by www. OpenWeatherMap.org

Overall



The northern hemisphere is tilted away from the sun during the November, so in general, it experiences colder temperatures than the southern hemisphere. However, many other factors can affect the temperature, such as altitude or proximity to large bodies of water.





Northern Cities



In the northern hemisphere, latitude does appear to be a good predictor of temperature. Nearly 75% of the variation in temperature could be attributed to latitude.





Southern Cities



In the souther hemisphere, latitude not as good of a predictor of temperature. Only 46% of the variation in temperature could be attributed to latitude. This is likely do to the higher ratio of ocean to land in the southern hemisphere.






Overall



It is difficult to say for certain whether the northern or southern hemisphere is generally more humid in November, as humidity can vary depending on a number of factors, including temperature, air pressure, and precipitation. However, the northern hemisphere is likely to be more humid in November than the southern hemisphere, due to the difference in land and water distribution between the two hemispheres. The northern hemisphere has a larger landmass and a smaller ocean surface area, which can lead to higher humidity levels. .





Northern Cities



In the northern hemisphere, latitude does not appear to be a good predictor of humidity. Only 5% of the variation in humidity could be attributed to latitude.





Southern Cities



In the southern hemisphere, latitude does not appear to be a good predictor of humidity. Only 7% of the variation in humidity could be attributed to latitude.






Overall



It is difficult to say for certain whether the northern hemisphere is generally more cloudy in the winter, as cloudiness can vary depending on a number of factors, including the time of year, the local weather conditions, and the presence of nearby bodies of water. In general, however, the northern hemisphere is likely to experience more clouds in the winter than in the summer. During the winter months the sun's rays hit the Earth's surface at a more oblique angle, resulting in more variable weather patterns and more clouds in the sky. However, this is just a general trend, and there can be many exceptions to this rule depending on the specific location and weather conditions.





Northern Cities



In the northern hemisphere, latitude does not appear to be a good predictor of cloudiness. Only 3% of the variation in cloudiness could be attributed to latitude. However, on October 31, there were more cities with 100% cloud coverage than there are with 0% cloud coverage.





Southern Cities



In the southern hemisphere, latitude does not appear to be a good predictor of cloudiness. Only 3% of the variation in cloudiness could be attributed to latitude. However, on October 31, there were more cities with 100% cloud coverage than there are with 0% cloud coverage..
